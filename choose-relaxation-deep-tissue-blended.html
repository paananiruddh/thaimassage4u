How to choose between Relaxation, Deep Tissue, and Blended massage

If you are unsure what to book, start with your goal for this session. Do you want to switch off, focus on stubborn tension, or combine both? This guide keeps the decision practical and clear.

Start with your session goal

Most people do not need technical language to choose well. A simple goal is enough: unwind, target specific tight areas, or balance both in one session.

When Relaxation usually fits best

Relaxation is useful when your week feels mentally and physically overloaded and your priority is to leave feeling calmer. Pressure is generally gentler and the pacing is steadier.

When Deep Tissue usually fits best

Deep tissue is usually the better fit when you want focused attention on persistent tight areas such as shoulders, upper back, hips, or legs. The goal is targeted work, not simply stronger pressure.

When a Blended session is the practical choice

Blended sessions work well when you want both decompression and some focused work in key areas. It is a useful option when you do not want to overthink categories.

Simple decision checklist before you enquire

  • What should feel better after this session?
  • Do you want gentle pacing, targeted focus, or a mix?
  • Which one or two areas matter most today?
