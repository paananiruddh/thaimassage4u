How to describe pressure preferences before you book

Pressure preference is one of the most useful details to share before booking. You do not need perfect wording; a clear description of comfort level is enough to improve session fit.

Use simple pressure language

Plain terms are usually best: lighter, moderate, or firmer. You can also describe what you want to avoid, such as very deep pressure in a sensitive area.

Match pressure to session goal

If your main goal is to unwind, gentler to moderate pressure often works better. If your goal is focused tension work, moderate to firmer pressure may be more suitable when kept manageable.

What to include in your enquiry

  • Preferred service type
  • Pressure preference in plain language
  • Any area that needs a gentler approach

FAQ

Do I need to choose one pressure for the whole session?

No. It is reasonable to ask for different pressure levels in different areas.

Should I mention comfort concerns before booking?

Yes. Mentioning comfort preferences early helps set clearer expectations.
