Practical questions

A FAQ page that answers the questions people actually have.

Strong support pages do more than fill out a menu. They remove hesitation, make the site easier to trust, and give search engines more context about what the business does and how it is presented.

How should someone choose between the services?

If the priority is to slow down and decompress, the gentler service description on the services page is usually the right place to start. If the issue is built-up tension through neck, shoulders, back, or legs, the more focused treatment description makes more sense. A blended session suits people who want both release and relaxation without overthinking the menu.

Why keep the suburb pages limited?

Because a smaller set of stronger pages is easier to maintain and more believable. Thin location pages can drag down the feel of the whole site. Carefully chosen pages with distinct tone do the opposite.

Is the site ready for advertising and affiliate content?

Yes. It includes the support pages that make that far more realistic: contact, about, FAQ, privacy, affiliate disclosure, sitemap, robots, and ads.txt.

Can this site be grown later?

Yes. The structure leaves room for service expansion, gift-card pages, booking integrations, editorial guides, or monetised recommendation pages without forcing you to rebuild the foundation.

Why does the copy avoid over-promising?

Because restraint reads as professional. It also tends to age better, especially if the site is positioned as a sellable digital asset later.

What helps most with AdSense approval?

Original content, complete support pages, visible navigation, a sensible number of pages, and a site that looks like it exists for readers rather than for ad slots alone.
