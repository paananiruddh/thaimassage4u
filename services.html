Service overview

Massage services explained in plain language

This page is designed to help you choose the session that matches how your body feels today. If you want to unwind, release stubborn tension, or combine both approaches, the guide below gives you a practical starting point.

Relaxation massage

A slower-paced session focused on calming the body and easing general tightness. Pressure is usually light to medium and adjusted to your comfort level.

This option suits people who feel mentally overloaded, sleep-poor, or physically fatigued and mainly want to leave feeling settled.

Deep tissue work

A targeted style for persistent tension in areas such as neck, shoulders, back, hips, or legs. Pressure is purposeful and managed so the session remains productive and tolerable.

If you spend long hours at a desk, train regularly, or feel ongoing stiffness, this category is often the best place to start.

Signature blended session

A combination approach that starts gently, then spends focused time on the areas that need more work before finishing at an easier pace.

This is a useful option if you want both relaxation and targeted pressure without committing to one style only.

How to choose the right session

Describe how you feel rather than trying to choose a technical label. For example: "I need to switch off," "my shoulders are very tight," or "I want a mix of both." That makes it easier to match the session style to your needs.

If time is limited, focusing on one or two priority areas is usually better than trying to cover everything quickly.
