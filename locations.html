Why a polished locations hub works better

Good suburb pages do not only chase a keyword. They help someone picture the fit. Armadale and Toorak should not sound like Carlton or Brunswick. The language should reflect the tempo of the area, the type of day someone there might be having, and what kind of treatment they are likely to respond to. That creates a much more believable page than swapping a suburb name into fixed paragraphs.

From an SEO standpoint, a smaller set of stronger pages also sends a cleaner signal. The site is easier to crawl, easier to understand, and much easier to maintain well over time.

What the suburb pages are trying to achieve Enough specificity that a local reader feels the page has a point of view, while still keeping the structure consistent enough that the whole site feels coherent.
